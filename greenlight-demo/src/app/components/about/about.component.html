How GreenLight works

  • After you select the permit or license you need, GreenLight evaluates what prerequisites are needed to acquire that permit or license (credential).
  • GreenLight repeats the process for each prerequisite credential until all the necessary permits and licenses are determined.
  • GreenLight then presents a map of the credentials needed and the order of acquisition necessary to meet the prerequisites, starting from the credentials that have no prerequisites.
  • For each credential, GreenLight indicates who the issuer is.

What do the symbols mean?

And the colours?

Green
Green signifies that you need and have this credential already.
Yellow
Yellow signifies that you need this credential but you don't have it yet; you have the dependencies to go for it so go get it!
Red
Red signifies that you need this credential but you don't have it. You don't have the dependencies for it so you cannot apply for it.

Example: Let's say you have a restaurant and you want to apply for a liquor license. GreenLight tells you in one easy step that:

  • You are a registered corporation.
  • You have WorkSafe BC coverage.
  • You have a PST number.
  • You do not have an operating permit, but you can apply for one./li>
  • Without the operating permit, you cannot apply for a liquor license.

GreenLight has saved you the time and hassle of trying to figure out what steps need to be completed and in what order when it comes to getting a liquor license - across multiple issuers and jurisdictions!
